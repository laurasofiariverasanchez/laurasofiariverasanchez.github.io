Laura Sofía Rivera Sánchez

About

I am a PhD student in Political Science at the University of Toronto, working at the intersection of comparative politics, political theory, and critical migration studies. My dissertation examines how rural transit communities along the migrant trail in the Americas are reconfigured by the shifting presence and, increasingly, absence, of undocumented migrants. Drawing on ongoing ethnographic fieldwork in General Farfán (Ecuador), Acandí (Colombia), Yaviza (Panama), and Bethel (Guatemala), I develop affect as a theoretical and methodological register for understanding how local economies, forms of belonging, and political imaginaries transform under shifting border regimes.

My research is currently supported by a Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada Doctoral Fellowship (SSHRC CGS-D) and by an Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS). I am currently working as a research assistant on the Bridging Divides project at the University of Toronto and Regimes of Closure and Mobility Aspiration at York University.

Before my doctoral studies, I completed an MA in Political Science at Toronto, a BA Honours in Political Science at Concordia, and a BSc Honours in Mathematics with a Minor in Physics at McGill. I have taught as a teaching assistant in courses on Latin American politics, gender and development, cities and citizenship, and comparative institutions.
